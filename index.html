01 / The Science Vestibular Sleep Induction

The inner ear holds the mechanism for infant sleep.

Calcium carbonate crystals — otoliths — sit in two tiny organs of the inner ear: the saccule and the utricle. They detect linear acceleration. When motion engages them at a specific therapeutic threshold, the brain transitions toward sleep.

For decades, parents have intuitively known that motion soothes infants — rocking, walking, driving. The mechanism behind that intuition is now established. Kompotis et al. (2019) identified the precise vestibular threshold at which otolith-mediated linear acceleration induces sleep: ≥25 cm/s² at a frequency of 0.25 Hz.

Every smart bassinet currently in market — SNOO, Cradlewise, Maxi-Cosi Starling, Elvie Rise, HALO BassiNest 3.0 — delivers motion. None has been engineered to a published, peer-reviewed acceleration target. They rock infants because rocking calms infants.

"Linear acceleration acting on the otolith organs is sufficient to induce sleep onset in infant mammals."

Kompotis et al., Current Biology (2019)

Vestibrum was founded to close that gap — to engineer infant sleep technology around the actual mechanism rather than around the symptom. The patent-pending platform is built on a true three-axis gimbal mechanism delivering precisely-calibrated lateral translation, paired with a closed-loop adaptive AI protocol that teaches infants to self-soothe rather than escalating stimulation when they cry.

02 / The Product

Otoli.

by Vestibrum

A smart sleep system that grows with the child — from birth through 3+ years.

Otoli is a modular smart infant sleep system: a 3-in-1 platform that transitions from bassinet to crib to toddler bed on a shared mechanism, shared software, and shared sensor stack.

At its core, the patent-pending Smart Base delivers three-axis vestibular motion engineered to a specific therapeutic acceleration target, layered with stochastic vibrotactile stimulation grounded in the same peer-reviewed neuroscience.

Onboard sensors continuously monitor the infant's breath, heart rate, and movement — feeding an adaptive AI protocol that gradually reduces stimulation as the infant develops self-soothing capacity. Where every other smart bassinet escalates in response to crying, Otoli teaches independent sleep.
